Leading Causes of Death in the United States (2016)

According to the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC), the top three leading causes of death in the United States in 2016 were heart disease (635,260 deaths), cancer (598,038 deaths), and uninentional injuries (161,374 deaths). Unintentional injuries include car crashes, falls, accidental poisoning, and other unintended casualties.
